My 2022 Book Review

For my JavaScript-based interactive feature, I chose to create a presentation slide show of all the books I have read so far in 2022. I found inspiration for this feature from our Image Carousel Weekly Assignment and decided to riff off of that to create something I was proud of. I love reading and wanted to make a place to keep a list of books and ratings for friend/family recommendations, book clubs and more.


My Body, By Em Rata

5/5

I became obsessed with this book as soon as I started it and couldn't stop. I listened to it on Audible,

which Emily Ratajkowski herself narrates. I feel like with social media, we are given a perspective of

peoples lives through posts and stories but I feel like I really got to know Emily in this book,

her rise to fame, and all the travesties she faced. This book was filled with raw emotion, honest and

heart breaking truth telling that needs to be revealed more about being a young woman in the spotlight.

She talks about her relationships with her mother, being a mother, powerful men taking advantage of

women in the industry and her relationship with her body. It was beautiful and important and a must read.

Where the Crawdads Sing, by Delia Owens

-/5

This book currently does not have a rating because it is still in progress. So far, I have liked it. I would

recommend this book to anyone with an appreciation for nature, good storytelling and beauty, as well as

anyone who is looking for a bit of mystery threaded into their story. The imagery is phenomenal and it is

truly a story of survival. The timeline of the book is interesting, as it jumps from present day to the past and

paints the story of a murder. I have heard mixed reviews so I am intrigued to finish and will update when I'm done.

Also, it was just made into a movie and I am excited to finish this book so I can watch it.

Elvis and Me, By Priscilla Beaulieu Presley

4/5

I read this book after seeing the Elvis movie this summer and becoming slightly obsessed. I enjoyed it

very much as it is told by the famous Priscilla and tells the story of her and Elvis' epic romance.

Once again, I listened to this book on Audible and enjoyed listening to her recount her times with him

and all the emotions that came with it. I would definitley recommend to any Elvis fan as a necessary read. It made

me cry, laugh, and dance. I loved how it painted an honest picture of one of the biggest celebrity couples in

history. Reading it was like sitting down for coffee with Priscilla and listening to her wonderful stories.

Rules of Civility, By Amor Towles

5/5

This book was another one of my favorites of 2022. I listened to this book while driving my car out to Colorado

with my dad this past summer. It transports readers to another time and place: New York City in the 1930s. It is a coming

of age story that follows a girl in her early twenties and takes readers through every season of her life. It was

definitely a love letter to NYC and all the opportunities that present themselves just through people you meet and

adventures you find yourself pursuing. I found myself relating to it a lot as I was starting a new job and I just

loved it. Perfect score

Pride and Prejudice, By Jane Austen

5/5

Being a classic, I knew this book was going to be hard to get into but this is an amazing book. The enemies

to lovers trope always makes for an incredibly romantic read. However, this book is not just about the

romance, but Jane Austen's writing style. This book contains subtle humor, witty insights and her view of

society and people's morals. I think everyone should read this book at least once in their life. Being my

first classic, I watched the movie before and took annotations on the pages to help me comprehend the

older language but consider this as being one of the best books I have ever read. Obviously,

because it is Jane Austen.

Call Me By Your Name, By Andr√© Aciman

5/5

I read this book this past summer having just spent the semster in Florence, Italy and it made me

want to go back. This book takes place in nothern Italy in the mid eighties during summertime.

It was very enjoyable and I loved how poetic the author's language was in describing different

aspects of the book, including the scenery, romance and relationships. It was an amazing story

of love and finding yourself. The ending was heart wrenching and portrayed the true tragedy of

moving on from a pivotal love and being forced to live in that new reality. Although painful,

true love is powerful and beautiful.

A Flicker in the Dark, By Stacy Willingham

3/5

This is a great suspense thriller that follows the daughter of a serial killer. The story is told through

anxious first-person narration in the present with flashbacks to her childhood, which builds up the tension well.

The author definitely puts you through a hauntingly compelling journey. While this book did keep me on the edge

of my seat and I was guessing what was going to happen up until the end, it received a sub-par rating because

the ending was not great and left a lot of questions unanswered.

Tomorrow and Tomorrow and Tomorrow, By Gabrielle Zevin

5/5

This was the most brilliant book I have read all year. I found myself absorbed in a fictional world with

not only excellent characters and plot, but a scavenger hunt of plays on perspective, voice and setting.

This book was very nostalgic and read more like a play than a novel. The writing was phenomenal

and examined life through an interesting lens of starting over when life presents us with difficult

situations. So good and so beautiful, would highly recommend.

The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo, By Taylor Jenkins Reid

3/5

This book was recommended to me by seemingly the entire world. Everyone raved about how good it

was, how it was the best book of 2021 and 2022, etc. I definitely liked it and the old bombshell

Hollywood aesthetic but thought it was a little overrated. It took me longer than expected to read

because it got a little old and slow at some points but other than that, it was an epic love story

filled with romance and tragedy. The characters were fabulous and there is a big plot twist.
